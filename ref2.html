  2. Week Two

    I loved the way the speaker represented or empathised with the emotions of a parent and a human being together. I learnt from the video not only that negative comments or conversations discourages people but also that positive conversation can even change someone perspective over something and as per the video you can make an impact even on a stranger.   3. Week Three

    This is an interpretation of the conversations I had with my brother . So,the message that I want to convey is about a visit to a museum. And the option for encode in this case is verbal and the channel is mobile phone . Then when my brother hears me and decodes the message he takes decision whether he wants to go or not then he tells me his feedback that he is ready to go. Now, the last element is noise, in this case the noises can be if he doesn’t pick up my call or bad network . This is one of my communication cycle.

    Our instructors’s both verbal and non-verbal aspects of communication are very interesting, friendly and amazing. Yes, they do compliment each other. No I don’t think there was any disconnection between the verbal and non verbal communication .The way of verbal communication is obviously more interactive. The positive things that I noticed is the way our instructor talks ,asks questions, explains and discusses things is very friendly and comfortable.

  4. Week Four

    This documentary is fantastic. The actual video clips grab everyone's attention and make the learning relevant and applicable. I would love to see an updated version on this. Fascinating!

    Don't we LOVE the kind of information that makes us sit up, look at each other and say, "WOW, that's INTERESTING"? This documentary is SO informative! we will not be a Body Language expert after watching this, but we are informed of almost every kind of non-verbal means of communication we let slip from how we walk, shake hands, gesture with our eyebrows, stand next to someone ... and the list goes on. We learned that 93% of all communication is NON-VERBAL, so it is pretty important to know what to look out for in a potential spouse, employee or political representative!

    There are some interesting, historic clips, and coverage of a few scientific experiments, the coverage is a bit "lite". There are a few too many "experts" analyzing footage of famous liars, implying that nonverbal communication is actually a language that can be accurately and professionally decoded.

    I have an avid interest in body language, especially as it relates to public speaking. I had high hopes that I would be able to recommend this video to my friends. I thought that the the visual nature of a video would help them to grasp the importance of body language faster.
